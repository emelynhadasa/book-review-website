Reviews For Perspectives

Many of us insist that the outside world is an obstacle to our performance, both in terms of work, education, and personal improvisation. It is yourself. In career preparation, the ego triggers us to show off a raw process or plan towards a goal, instead of showing off the results. Amid success, the ego blinds us from mistakes and self-improvement due to a lazy mentality, high satisfaction, and a fixed mindset. In failure, the ego hyperboles the effects of failure and prevents us from getting up again.

This book draws on stories and events from figures such as George Marshall, Katharine Graham, Bill Belihick, and others who achieved success by conquering their egos. Their strategies and tactics can also be ours.

How Is The Contents?

The plot is great flowy, adapted to the lives and goals of people generally so it's greatly related.
